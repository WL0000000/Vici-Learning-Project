# Vici Learning Integration Dashboard | Group 15

**Client:** Sarah Alhower, Vici Learning · **Course:** CMPT 276

## Abstract

The Vici Learning Integration Dashboard is a browser-based, staff-facing web application that unifies the daily operations of Vici Learning, a private tutoring company. Vici currently runs its business across several disconnected tools: SimplyBook.me handles session booking, Brevo manages customer relationships and email, Notion holds the tutor database, and a collection of manual spreadsheets ties everything together. Because none of these systems talk to one another, staff spend several hours every week exporting reports, reconciling them by hand, and manually chasing the families who have not booked or paid. Our application pulls booking and client information out of these systems into one secure dashboard, analyzes that data to surface the tasks that actually need attention, and triggers timely follow-up communication with families. The result is a single source of truth that removes repetitive manual work, reduces the errors that creep in when data is copied between tools by hand, and scales with the company as it grows.

## The Problem

Vici Learning operates with a fragmented toolchain in which each platform owns a different slice of the business and no platform sees the whole picture. To answer a simple question such as which students have stopped booking, or how many sessions are scheduled for next week, a staff member must log in to multiple systems, export spreadsheets, and combine them manually in Excel. This reconciliation work consumes roughly four to five hours per week and is highly error-prone. The clearest example is the way each family is assigned a unique account identifier that is generated by hand in Brevo and then copied into SimplyBook.me; a single mistyped identifier silently breaks the link between a family's booking history and their billing record. These problems are tolerable today, when the company serves between fifty and seventy students, but they do not scale. Vici expects to reach two to three hundred students in the short term and aims for more than a thousand in the long term, at which point manual reconciliation becomes impossible to sustain.

## How the Problem Is Solved Today

At present the problem is solved almost entirely through manual effort. Staff treat SimplyBook.me, Brevo, Notion, and Excel as separate islands, moving data between them by exporting files and re-entering values. Deciding which families to contact about an unbooked or unpaid session is done by eye, and the follow-up emails are written and sent one at a time.

A number of commercial products attempt to solve this class of problem, including all-in-one tutoring management suites such as TutorCruncher, Teachworks, and Oases. These platforms are capable, but they are a poor fit for Vici for several reasons. Adopting one would mean abandoning the SimplyBook.me and Brevo subscriptions the company already pays for and is comfortable using, migrating historical data into a new system, and retraining staff. It would also lock the business into a single vendor and introduce recurring per-seat or per-student fees, and the client has explicitly stated that the project must run on free tiers and existing subscriptions with no additional budget. In other words, the existing systems either solve the problem manually or solve it by replacing everything the client already has. Our application takes a different position: rather than competing with the tools Vici already relies on, it sits on top of them and coordinates them, delivering the benefits of an integrated platform without the cost and disruption of switching.

## Our Solution

The dashboard follows a synchronize-to-local-database architecture so that it never depends on a live external call while a staff member is using a page. Scheduled background jobs pull bookings, clients, services, and tutors from SimplyBook.me and normalize them into our own PostgreSQL database. A rules engine then analyzes this local data to produce a list of actionable tasks, such as students who have lapsed beyond a configurable window, families who remain unpaid after a set number of sessions, low booking volume for the coming week, and unusual cancellation activity. Every dashboard page reads only from the local database, which keeps the interface fast, safe from external rate limits, and usable even when an upstream service is temporarily unavailable. Finally, the application acts on those tasks by triggering communication through Brevo, syncing contact and booking data back into the customer relationship manager and sending templated emails. In keeping with the client's priorities, routine messages such as session and payment reminders may be sent automatically, while anything that is personal in nature is placed in a review queue for a human to approve before it goes out, and every email that is sent is recorded in an audit log.

## Target Audience

The application is an internal tool for the administrative and operations staff of Vici Learning, who need a single place to monitor bookings, identify follow-up work, and manage outbound communication. It is explicitly not a public-facing product: parents, students, and clients never receive access to it. The design anticipates a second class of user in the future, namely tutors, who would be given view-only access scoped to their own students, but in the current scope the primary audience is the company's administrators.

## Educational Value Versus Entertainment

This project is neither a game nor an entertainment product; it is a business productivity and operations tool. Its purpose is to make the work of running an education business measurably easier by eliminating hours of repetitive manual reconciliation, reducing the data-entry errors that damage customer relationships, and giving staff the information they need to act before a student lapses or a payment is missed. In that sense it improves quality of life for the people who use it and, indirectly, supports the educational mission of the company they work for.

## External Web API

The project satisfies the requirement to consume a web REST API by gathering data directly over HTTPS rather than relying on a prebuilt library that hides the underlying calls. Our primary integration is with SimplyBook.me, which exposes a JSON-RPC 2.0 web API. We implement our own client for this API using Spring's `RestClient` and Jackson, authenticating to obtain a token and then issuing raw HTTP requests for bookings, clients, services, and performers and parsing the responses ourselves. Because we construct and send these HTTP calls directly, this clearly meets the rule that we must use a web API rather than simply treating a dependency as one. The application also integrates with Brevo's REST API version 3 for contact synchronization and transactional email. All development is performed against free sandbox and trial accounts using generated mock data, and no sensitive payment information is ever entered, stored, or transmitted, in line with both the course guidance and Canadian privacy expectations for the client's real data.

## Scope

The scope of the project is a single internal web platform built from several substantial subsystems that share one database and one secure interface. Within scope are user authentication and role-based access, automated synchronization of data from SimplyBook.me, an analytics dashboard with filtering and a tutor drill-down view, a configurable rules engine that produces an inbox of actionable tasks, and a communication layer that sends and logs email through Brevo under human oversight. Deliberately out of scope are any public-facing or customer-facing pages, the processing of real payments, and any attempt to replace SimplyBook.me or Brevo themselves; the application orchestrates those services rather than reimplementing them.

## Epics

The project is best understood as one main platform composed of several large features, each of which contains many subproblems. We have identified five epics, which matches the size of our five-person team and gives each member ownership of one major feature.

**Authentication and role-based access control** establishes secure form-based login and the distinction between an administrator role that sees everything and a future tutor role with view-only access scoped to its own students. This epic delivers the login that is required for the first iteration and underpins the security of every other feature.

**SimplyBook.me integration and data synchronization** is the heart of the system and the epic that fulfils the web API requirement. It covers the hand-written JSON-RPC client, the scheduled jobs that pull and normalize bookings and contacts into the local database, and a sync-status page that lets staff confirm the data is current.

**Operations dashboard and analytics** turns the synchronized data into insight, presenting hours booked, cancellations, upcoming sessions, and active student counts through clear visualizations, with filters and the tutor drill-down view that the client identified as the single most valuable feature.

**Actionable tasks and the rules engine** analyzes the local data against admin-configurable thresholds to generate a prioritized inbox of follow-up work, flagging lapsed students, unpaid families, weak upcoming booking volume, and cancellation spikes.

**Brevo communication, automation, and audit logging** closes the loop by syncing contacts back into Brevo and sending templated emails, applying a per-message policy of automatic sending for routine reminders and a human review queue for anything personal, while writing every send to an audit log.

## Sufficiency of Work for Five Members

The amount of work is well matched to a five-person team because each of the five epics is a major feature in its own right rather than a minor task. Building a reliable client for an external API, designing a synchronization pipeline, creating an analytics dashboard, implementing a configurable rules engine, and building an audited communication system with selective automation are each substantial pieces of engineering, and they are interdependent enough to require genuine coordination. This gives every group member a clearly defined area of ownership while ensuring that the project as a whole is integrated rather than a collection of isolated parts.

## Tech Stack

The application is written in Java 21 on Spring Boot 3, built with Maven, and rendered server-side with Thymeleaf so that the interface is plain HTML and vanilla JavaScript with no frontend framework, in keeping with course guidance. Data is stored in PostgreSQL, which runs locally through Docker Compose, while tests run against an in-memory H2 database. The SimplyBook.me integration is a hand-written JSON-RPC client built on Spring's `RestClient`, and the Brevo integration uses its REST API for contacts and transactional email. Background work is handled by Spring's scheduling support, and security is provided by Spring Security with `ADMIN` and `TUTOR` roles.

## Team Workflow Rules

Everyone works on their own separate branch, and no one commits or pushes directly to the `main` branch. Only working, reviewed code is merged into `main`, pushes are communicated frequently, and everyone pulls whenever a teammate pushes so that local branches stay current and merge conflicts are avoided. Team members announce which feature or component they are working on so that two people do not edit the same area at once. Real client data and API keys are never committed; the team works exclusively with sandbox accounts and mock data.
